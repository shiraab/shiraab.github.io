Shira is currently studying Translation and Computer Science at Brown University. She writes and translates with Limited Connection Collective and The Legs of the Corduroy Pants. Right now, she is surviving a global pandemic (as are you!), having previously survived a year-long concussion. She is currently co-creating and managing Poems from a University Quarantine, a web-based collaborative writing platform and workshop. She makes websites, poems, and coffee in her free time, and is passionate about accessibility and ethics in computer science and in the world.

This site is currently being updated. Stay tuned!

After years of collaborating on experimental poetry projects, Rachel Landau and I (aka The Legs of the Corduroy Pants) founded and created Poems from a University Quarantine as a place to stay connected and creating during the long months away from school. As site manager, I manage its accessibility and organization. We ran a two-part workshop series over Zoom with the Brown Arts Initiative called POETRY IS FOR KITCHENS and are currently extending the project into a summer session curriculum for high school students.

We created a fully-integrated accessibility curriculum for an introductory web development course.

We gathered, analyzed, and visualized data based on a central question: did the scandals surrounding the 2016 election and big tech companies change tech PACs' donation patterns?

We worked with The Policy Lab to create a tool for Wagtail CMS-based sites to automatically submit published pages for translation through a third-party API.

Place-memory can be loosely described as the set of historical, cultural, and social ideas relating to a particular place. I planned, led, and conducted a semester-long foray into place-memory in literature and history in Paris, culminating in a French-language website.

We worked with an on-campus newspaper to build a map that allows site editors to add articles to a map, connecting writing to the place where it is based.
